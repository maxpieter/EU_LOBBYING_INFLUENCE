### Theme-Level Observations

A comparative analysis of amendment activity and lobbying focus reveals distinct patterns across policy themes. The "role of intermediaries and disintermediation" theme garnered the most amendments, with 372 changes proposed. This theme also saw some lobbying activity, with 3 recorded Commission meetings. In contrast, "offline functionality and accessibility" was a highly amended theme (229 amendments) but recorded no lobbying meetings with the Commission or Parliament. This suggests that parliamentary attention on this aspect may have been driven by other factors than disclosed lobbying.

Conversely, "innovation and competition in payments" was the most lobbied theme with 20 Commission meetings, and it also received substantial parliamentary attention with 182 amendments. "Interoperability and cross-border payments" also attracted significant lobbying (9 Commission meetings) but generated a comparatively lower number of amendments (96), suggesting limited parliamentary uptake of proposed changes on this theme relative to lobbying efforts.

### Limitations

This analysis is based solely on publicly disclosed lobbying meetings and amendments, meaning informal contacts and other forms of influence are not captured. Furthermore, while the analysis identifies associations and directional alignment, it does not establish causal relationships between lobbying activity and legislative outcomes.
